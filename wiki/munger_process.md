## Cleaning & Sanitization

- **Sanitizer**: Oxisan (similar to Star San) for fermenter, hoses, kegs, and all contact surfaces. 70% alcohol for spot sanitization.
- **Kettle**: Cleaned immediately after each brew session.
- **Kegs**: Scrubbed by hand, then soaked with PLC (Polvo Limpiador Cervecero, a brewing alkaline cleaner) solution.

## Storing Ingredients

Dry yeast: in freezer
Hops: in freezer
Malts: in plastic bags, room temperature

## Milling

With a stainless steel roller mill. Adjusting the gap between rollers. For BIAB I mill finer. For sparging I use a credit card thickness gap.

I mill the day before brew day and store it in a tightly sealed plastic bag.

## Water

I filter the water with a carbon filter, the day before. I leave it in the stainless steel brew kettle with a lid (50L, with a bottom tap and thermometer).

Total water volume is approximately 34 liters: around 23 liters for the main mash, and approximately 11 liters set aside for the mash out infusion.

## Mashing

On brew day I check the taste of the water and the pH using an ADWA AD12 pH meter.
I start heating the water and add the recipe salts. Usually CaCl and/or CaSO4.
I add citric acid (solid) according to the pH calculations.
I heat the water to approximately 74°C.
I add the mash bag and then the malts, mixing everything well.
I set aside part of the water in another aluminum pot.
The mash ends up fairly thin.
I stir every 10 minutes or so.
I generally mash for 50 minutes at a temperature between 64 and 70°C, depending on the recipe.
Then I add the water I set aside, brought to a boil, to do a mash out at 75°C.

## Lautering
At the end I remove the mash bag and place it on a rack over the mash pot.
I let it drain as much as possible and at the end I squeeze it a little.

I start heating over a gas burner on high heat until boiling.

## Boil
I measure pre-boil gravity with a hydrometer.
I boil for approximately 60 minutes. I add hops according to the recipe.
I add Irish moss and yeast nutrient (Servomyces) in the last 5 minutes of the boil.

## Whirlpool

I turn off the heat and do a whirlpool (by stirring), leaving it for 10 minutes with the lid on.

## Chilling

Chilled with an aluminum coil chiller. Tap water comes in and passes through another coil inside a fridge with frozen water bottles.

I chill for approximately 1 hour and manage to bring it down to around 25-28°C.

## Fermentation

From there I transfer to my fermenter, from the bottom tap of the kettle via a hose. My fermenter is a 20-liter Cornelius keg with a floating dip tube. Typical volume into the fermenter is 16-17 liters.

I measure OG with a hydrometer after transferring.

I put the keg in a fridge at 5°C to cool down to pitching temperature.
For example, for a lager:
 - it takes 3 hours to drop from 25 to 17°C where I pitch (I directly add the dry yeast, 1 packet of 11g)
 - it takes another 3 hours to drop from 17 to 12°C, the temperature I then maintain for the primary fermentation

I pitch dry yeast directly into the keg, close the lid and agitate for 20 seconds

I monitor fermentation activity by counting seconds between bubbles in the blow-off tube, and take 2-3 hydrometer readings throughout fermentation to track and confirm completion.

After 7 days I raise the temperature to 16°C and leave it there for 3-4 days.

The blow-off is a tube connected to a ball lock connector on the keg, submerged in sanitizer water.
Sometimes I disconnect the blow off and attach a spunding valve at 15 psi.

## Dry Hopping (optional)

When the recipe calls for it, I dry hop at the end of primary fermentation. I open the Corny keg lid, add the hops, close the lid, then purge oxygen by adding and releasing CO2 three times.

## Cold Crash

On day 12 I lower the temperature to 1°C and leave it for 2 days.

Then I transfer to smaller 10-liter kegs.
I fill the kegs with sanitizer (Oxisan) and then purge them with CO2.
I fill from the fermenter dip tube to the small keg's tube (fills from the bottom up).
These kegs stay in the fridge (1°C) for a few more days before starting to drink.

**Carbonation**: Depending on the batch, I use one of three methods:
- Set-and-forget: connect CO2 at serving pressure and wait several days
- Burst carb: 40 psi for ~6 hours, then drop to serving pressure
- Agitation: shake/roll the keg while connected to CO2 to speed up absorption

**Fining agents** (optional):
- Gelatin: sometimes added for clarity
- SMB (sodium metabisulfite): sometimes added as an antioxidant

**Serving**: 1 bar (approximately 14.5 psi) at 3-4°C.
