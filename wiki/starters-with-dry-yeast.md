## Yeast Starter Growth Model

| Inoculation rate (B cells/L) | Growth factor | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| < 25 | ~6x | Maximum growth, but stressed yeast |
| 50 | ~4x | Sweet spot for healthy growth |
| 100 | ~2x | Diminishing returns |
| 200 | ~1.4x | Minimal growth |
| 400+ | ~1x | Essentially no growth |

### Assumptions baked into this table

- **Continuous agitation or stir plate.** Numbers assume the starter is on a stir plate spinning constantly. For intermittent shaking (like with a growler), multiply growth factor by ~0.7. For no agitation at all, multiply by ~0.5.
- **Starter gravity of 1.030–1.040.** Higher gravities (1.050+) reduce growth significantly due to osmotic stress and faster alcohol accumulation. Lower gravities (<1.025) don't provide enough nutrients.
- **Healthy ale yeast strain.** The model was developed primarily from ale yeast data. **Lager yeast typically grows ~20–30% less** at the same inoculation rate, so discount accordingly for W-34/70 and other lager strains.
- **Adequate oxygenation at pitch.** Assumes the starter wort was aerated (splashed, shaken, or oxygenated) before pitching yeast. Without initial O₂, growth drops significantly.
- **Sufficient nutrients.** Assumes DME-based wort or wort with added yeast nutrient. Pure sucrose or under-nourished wort grows fewer cells.
- **Fermentation temperature in the yeast's healthy growth range** (~18–22 °C for most strains, including lager yeast in a starter).
- **Fresh, viable yeast.** Growth factor applies to viable cells, not total cells. If your yeast is old or poorly stored, calculate starting from viable count, not the manufacturer's spec.
- **24–48 hour duration.** Numbers reflect the peak growth window. Stopping too early (<24 h) leaves growth on the table; going past 48 h doesn't add cells.
- **The numbers are approximations.** This is a curve-fit model, not a precise measurement. Real-world results vary ±20–30% depending on the specific strain, wort composition, and conditions. Use it for planning, not as a guarantee.
