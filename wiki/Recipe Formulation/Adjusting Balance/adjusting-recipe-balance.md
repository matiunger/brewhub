# Adjusting Balance

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Envisioning Your Beer. Part of [[recipe-formulation]]. (For adjusting balance in **finished** beer by blending, see [[final-adjustments]] in Finishing Beer.)*

Beyond the mechanics of formulation lie the **tradeoffs** for tuning a beer's overall balance — when scaling a recipe or nudging it toward your target. The sub-topics:

- **[[scaling-recipes]]** — making imperial/double or session versions without wrecking the balance (BU:GU, FG creep).
- **[[adjusting-final-gravity-and-attenuation]]** — sugars vs dextrins; body, sweetness, and refreshment.
- **[[balancing-bitterness-alcohol-and-tannins]]** — perceived vs measured bitterness; how age changes alcohol and tannins.
- **[[interactions]]** — why most changes ripple, and how to avoid fixing a problem twice.

## The Core Idea

Balance is about **perception**, not just numbers. The same component (bitterness, body, alcohol) is read differently depending on everything else in the beer — and on **age and condition**. When you change a recipe, change deliberately and watch for side effects.
