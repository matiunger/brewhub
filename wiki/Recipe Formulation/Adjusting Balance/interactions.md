# Interactions

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Envisioning Your Beer. Part of [[adjusting-recipe-balance]].*

When adjusting a recipe you can change several variables at once — but **be aware of how they interact.**

## The Classic "Fixing It Twice" Trap

Say a beer needs **more bitterness**. You could:

- increase **hop bitterness**, or
- increase **attenuation** to dry it out, or
- **lower the gravity** so it finishes lower.

Each shifts the **bitter-vs-sweet** balance. Do **two** of them (e.g. more IBUs *and* more attenuation) and you may **overshoot** your target.

## Principles

- **Don't assume you must control the most obvious variable.** To make a beer seem more bitter, raising IBUs isn't the only path — you care about **perceived bitterness in the overall balance**, achievable several ways.
- **Most changes ripple.** Go easy unless you understand them well.
- **Prefer changes that don't interact** with each other. When a change has side effects, pick the adjustment whose end state most closely matches your target.
- Always think about **what component creates the perception you want**, and keep them in balance per your recipe target.
