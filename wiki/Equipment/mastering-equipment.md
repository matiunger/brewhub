# Mastering Equipment

*Chapter 3 hub from Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer." You don't need a fancy, expensive, or automated system to brew award-winning beer — but you must **know your own system inside and out**: its strengths, weaknesses, and how it responds to different conditions.*

> *"Trust, but verify."* — observe how others' systems work, but **test their methods on your system** before accepting them as fact. Don't be a gear snob, and don't blindly adopt recommendations.

Strong frames mastering equipment as **three phases**, each a step up in skill:

1. **[[matching-equipment-to-the-task]]** — *selecting* gear (a basic skill). Form follows function: start from the task, then find the device.
2. **[[learning-your-system]]** — knowing how your system responds (a more advanced skill). Treat it as black boxes: predict outputs from inputs.
3. **[[optimizing-your-brewing]]** — doing the same tasks more efficiently and effectively (the sign of mastery — the "zen of brewing").
