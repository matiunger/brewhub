# Packaging Equipment

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[matching-equipment-to-the-task]]. For carbonation/kegging detail, see [[kegging]].*

Three areas of choice: **clarification, carbonation, and storage.**

## Clarification

Most clarifying needs **no extra gear** beyond normal racking equipment. To go further, a **plate filter** fits inline in a keg system and pushes beer into a target keg. Strong owns one but uses it **only for very pale beers** where clarity is at a premium, and only if fining failed — **filtering wastes some beer and pads are expensive.**

## Bottling (minimal gear)

Bottling bucket · racking cane · siphon tube · bottle filler — plus a **capper** (handheld **wing** vs **bench**; he prefers the **bench capper** for larger runs). Worth owning even if you keg, for **bottling from kegs** for competitions or gifts.

## Kegging (more gear)

- **Enough kegs** to hold all your beer. **Standardize on one fitting system** (ball-locks are most common) so you don't maintain multiple parts.
- Beer and gas fittings, hoses, clamps to the **CO₂ tank**; dispensing needs CO₂, beer line, clamps, and taps.
- **Maintenance/repair gear:** long thin brushes, replacement gaskets/O-rings, poppet valves, relief valves, post-removal tools.
- **Transferring from kegs:** beer-to-beer **keg jumpers** (also good for cleaning), a filter system, or a **counter-pressure filler / BeerGun**.

> Kegging is **highly personalized** — with a stock of basic parts (O-rings, washers, poppets, fittings) you can build whatever device you need or replace failed components. Keep an ample supply on hand.
