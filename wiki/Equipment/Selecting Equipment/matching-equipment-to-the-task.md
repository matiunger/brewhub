# Matching Equipment to the Task

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[mastering-equipment]].*

**Form follows function.** Start with what you need to accomplish, then find the device that best meets the need. There are always tradeoffs — usually cost — but keep the **task foremost** and you'll assemble and upgrade smoothly.

## Principles

- **Don't buy gear without knowing how you'll use it.** Equipment needn't be brewing-specific — a kitchen or hardware item may be better for the task, or adaptable.
- **Keep an open mind** — don't be limited by what one store sells.
- **One tool for multiple tasks is fine** — but understand the impact: Is throughput limited? Can it still be cleaned easily? Can you still multitask? Consider equipment **together with your process choices**.
- **Cost is an implicit factor in every choice.** A brewer with limited resources must decide where to spend for maximum payback — not everyone can choose on technical merit alone.
