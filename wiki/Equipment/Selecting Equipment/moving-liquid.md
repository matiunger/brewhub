# Moving Liquid

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[matching-equipment-to-the-task]].*

Water and wort move between vessels by **manual lifting, gravity, or pumps**. Along with the number of vessels, **this choice drives your whole system design.** View brewing as discrete phases handled separately, or as a continuous linked process.

The key transfer points: water → hot liquor tank, liquor → mash tun, sparge water → mash tun, lauter runoff → kettle, boiled wort → fermenter.

## Three Approaches

- **Gravity (sculpture):** each stage sits higher than the next. Some systems tier every phase; others have two levels and raise liquid manually at each step.
- **Manual + grants:** collect liquid in a grant, lift to the next stage.
- **Pumps:** move liquid in any configuration, and run **two tasks at once**.

## What to Evaluate

| Factor | Questions |
|--------|-----------|
| **Safety** | Risk of burns, electrocution, or back injury from lifting? |
| **Automation** | Work at each step, or automated? |
| **Effort** | Mechanical lift vs manual lifting |
| **Ease of use** | Pumps self-priming? Handle full temperature range? One-hand controls nearby? Heat-rated hoses? |
| **Flow rate** | Adjustable? Max rate? Shutoff? Full-bore connectors? |
| **Integration** | Dedicated vs reconfigurable between phases |

## Strong's Setup

Used **gravity for years** before automating (he never felt safe carrying near-boiling water up ladders to sparge). Now runs **two March pumps**, so he can pump sparge water onto the mash *and* mash runoff into the kettle simultaneously.

- March pumps handle near-boiling temps but are **not self-priming** → mount low or make removable.
- **Flow controlled by valves**, not the pumps.
- Nothing lifts above chest height (only the initial HLT fill); pumps and hoses move around the system on **quick-connects**.
