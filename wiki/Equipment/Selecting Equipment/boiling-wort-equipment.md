# Boiling Wort — The Kettle

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[matching-equipment-to-the-task]]. For boil technique, see [[managing-the-boil]].*

Requirements are simple — but **size for the full preboil volume**.

## Sizing

- Hold the complete **preboil volume**, which can be **up to 3 gal (11.4 L) more** than your target — accounting for **boil expansion** and the **froth before hot break**.
- A **half-barrel keg** is just enough for a vigorous **10-gal (37.9-L)** batch.
- The boil kettle **must be direct-fired**.

## Sharing Vessels (simpler systems)

You *can* use the boil kettle as your hot liquor tank — **if** you can store some boiling water during the sparge. But it's harder and means moving sizeable amounts of boiling water, so **think hard** before choosing it.

> Often simpler: use a **smaller dedicated HLT** and **boil water in batches**, possibly reusing an old pot (your previous kettle, or one "borrowed" from the kitchen).
