# Lautering — Tun, False Bottoms & Sparge Gear

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[matching-equipment-to-the-task]]. For lautering technique, see [[lautering]] and [[lautering-options]].*

First decision: **same vessel as the mash, or a separate lauter tun?** Well-designed complete systems usually combine them. The key concern: **how manipulating grain during the mash affects lautering** — if you must stir, will it clog your lautering system?

## Strong's Evolution (and the stuck-sparge lesson)

- **Gott cooler + Phil's Phalse Bottom:** frequent **stuck sparges**. Reduced by holding the false bottom down with the mash paddle while adding grain, then **underletting** water — but stirring easily forced grain *under* the false bottom, clogging the outlet.
- **Rectangular cooler to mash, Gott to lauter:** better lautering (no stirring once in the lauter tun), fewer stuck sparges — but **lost temperature** moving vessels, needing a near-boiling mash-out to recover.
- **Now — three-vessel system:** a **tight-fitting false bottom that doesn't float** eliminates both problems.

> Alternative pickups that work well: a **slotted-pipe** or **bazooka-screen** system in a rectangular cooler. Without a false bottom, stirring is less of a problem and these clog less.

## Vorlauf & Sparge Gear

- **Vorlauf** is easy on any system (little volume): run off into a saucepan/grant and gently pour back onto the bed (or pump it back), repeating until the outflow runs clear. See [[preparing-to-sparge]].
- **Sparge gear depends on method:**
  - **Batch sparge** — easy anywhere: add water, drain, repeat. Best choice if your transfers aren't automated. See [[lautering-options]] and [[batch-sparging-analysis]].
  - **Continuous sparge** — balance inflow/outflow to keep the water level steady. Strong **dislikes spray systems that aerate** the water; he keeps the level ~1 inch above the bed and adds gently. If water isn't going directly onto the grain, how it arrives doesn't matter.
