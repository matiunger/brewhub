# Planning Your Brew Calendar

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Equipment. Part of [[optimizing-your-brewing]].*

The obvious reason to plan a calendar: **have a beer ready by a target date**, allowing for the style's conditioning time — or, going further, ready **at peak flavor** (the essence of planning for competitions). But there are other practical drivers for the **order** you brew in.

## What Drives the Order

- **Yeast reuse** — arrange a season's recipes so you can **repitch** from one batch to the next. Allow enough time for full fermentation, racking, and yeast cleaning before repitching; racking to secondary buys conditioning time. See [[preparing-yeast]].
- **Equipment availability** — usually **fermenters** are the bottleneck, but kegs or packaging gear can constrain you too; a piece needing repair may rule some styles in or out.
- **Seasonal ingredients** — seasonal **yeast strains** (mitigate by banking them yourself — see [[yeast-bank-contents]]); **fresh hop harvest** (the extreme: wet-hop beers); or simply a supplier being **out of stock** so you must mail-order — you may restructure around when ingredients (or new equipment) arrive.
- **Using opened ingredients** — brew several successive beers from **one sack of malt** (freshest grain + experience with it under different conditions) or an **opened bag of whole hops**, especially if you can't reseal it well.

> None of these are contrived — ignore them and your schedule suffers. A little advance planning avoids the hassles and wasted time.
