# Finishing Beer

*Chapter 8 hub from Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer." Welcome to the **cold side** — turning fermented beer into something you serve. The goals: **mature the flavors, clarify the beer, and achieve stability** — without ruining it through oxidation or infection.*

> *"All's well that ends."*

The cold side has different concerns than the hot side. Two rules dominate everything here: **keep oxygen out after fermentation** and **store cold**.

## The Cold-Side Flow

1. **[[beer-stability]]** — what preserves (and destroys) beer: oxygen, temperature, pH.
2. **[[conditioning]]** — reducing green-beer flavors; the "secondary" myth.
3. **[[lagering]]** — cold conditioning for mellow, clean lager character.
4. **[[clarifying]]** — natural settling, finings, the two-keg jumper trick.
5. **[[stabilizing]]** — preventing protein/tannin chill haze.
6. **[[the-science-of-foam]]** — formation vs. retention, and why foam is a sensitive indicator of fermentation and process cleanliness.
7. **[[carbonating-and-packaging]]** · **[[oxygen-control-when-filling]]** · **[[cask-conditioning]]** — getting it into the package.
