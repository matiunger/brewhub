# Adjusting Balance

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Finishing Beer. Part of [[final-adjustments]].*

> The book's dedicated **Blending** section is intentionally **excluded** from this wiki. This page covers only the balance-correction idea from "Final Adjustments."

If a finished beer lacks a key component, you can nudge its balance by **blending in a little of another homebrew**:

| Beer needs | Blend in a little… |
|------------|--------------------|
| More hop character | IPA |
| Richer malt character | Scotch ale or doppelbock |
| Less sweetness | A drier or hoppier beer |

**Tips:**

- **Don't throw out "off-balance" batches** (too malty, hoppy, roasty, caramelly, or estery) — **save them for blending.**
- Even **blending two batches of the same beer** can add complexity.
- Expect to run **several small-scale trials** to get the ratio right before committing.
