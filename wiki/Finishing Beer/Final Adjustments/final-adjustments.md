# Final Adjustments

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Finishing Beer. Part of [[finishing-beer]].*

A good chef tastes every dish before it leaves the kitchen and **fixes** what's wrong — doesn't serve it flawed, doesn't dump it unless it's unrecoverable. Same with beer: you shouldn't dump a salvageable batch, nor resign yourself to drinking mediocrity. **Learn to fix it.**

## Triage First

Quickly sample to triage: **ready to serve, fatally flawed, or fixable?** For the fixable third path, use structured tasting to **identify every technical and style flaw, quantify each, and prioritize** — which are most severe, and which fixes would most improve the beer? Most **balance** issues and some minor technical errors are correctable; some faults can be **masked** to improve perception.

## Fatal Faults (dump it)

In noticeable quantity: **infection, chlorophenolic (medicinal), light-struck (skunky), metallic, papery oxidation, some phenolics, burned/scorched, autolyzed, vegetal.**

> One surprise Strong **wouldn't** immediately dump: **vinegary** — depending on the base style it might become something interesting. He medaled at the 2010 NHC with an old doppelbock that developed an **acetic** infection, entered as a Flanders Brown.

Masking off-flavors in a pinch is possible but undesirable (the Chicago NHC bourbon-barrel imperial stout with a lactic infection was dosed with calcium carbonate, lactose, and vanilla — better than dumping 53 gallons, but no award winner).

## Faults That Can Be Fixed or Improved

| Fault | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Acetaldehyde, diacetyl, esters | **Additional fermentation** — [[kraeusening]] is faster than repitching (active yeast + sugar cleans up) |
| Tannin-related: astringency, harshness, grassy | **Fine with Polyclar** (rack after — not food-safe) |
| Alcohol heat, sulfur, yeasty | **Age** — smooths out |
| Esters, fusels, sourness | May **change/improve with age** as they oxidize |
| Low hops/bitterness, too sweet, style/balance | **Adjust directly, balance, or blend** — see [[adjusting-balance]] |

Recognizing what to save vs restart is a skill built with experience.

## Where to Make Adjustments

- **Kegged beer is easiest** — adjust directly in the keg (after testing on a small scale first).
- **Bottled beer** (e.g. for competition) — decant into a 2-L soda bottle, adjust, rebottle.
- Always transfer into **sanitized** containers with **CO₂ displacing air**.
