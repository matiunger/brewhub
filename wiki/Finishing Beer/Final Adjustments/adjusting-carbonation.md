# Adjusting Carbonation

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Finishing Beer. Part of [[final-adjustments]]. See also [[carbonating-and-packaging]].*

## Undercarbonated

- **Kegged:** simply **force carbonate longer**.
- **Bottled / pulled for adjustment:** pour into a 2-L soda bottle, fit a **carbonator cap**, chill to **near freezing**, and **shake CO₂ in at 30 psi for 10–15 seconds**.

## Overcarbonated

- **Mild:** keep at **room temperature** and **vent pressure every hour** until right; gently swirl the keg/bottle to speed it.
- **Massively over (kegged):** open the keg, insert a **mix-stir on a drill** **below the surface** and drive out carbonation (what a mix-stir is designed for — degassing wine). **Stay under the surface** so you don't aerate, and **don't form a vortex**.
