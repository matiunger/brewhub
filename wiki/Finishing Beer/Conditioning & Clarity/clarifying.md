# Clarifying

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Finishing Beer. Part of [[finishing-beer]].*

Clarification can happen in the primary, or during conditioning/lagering. **Longer, colder conditioning aids yeast sedimentation.** Many brewers serve unfiltered and pick **flocculent yeast strains** to help.

> **Carbonate *after* clarifying** — carbonation interferes with the beer dropping bright and with filtering.

## Natural Clarification (Strong's default)

Keg, **crash cool** (warm room → near freezing), and leave alone for weeks to settle.

**The two-keg jumper trick:**
1. Pour until the beer runs bright.
2. Jumper to a sanitized, **CO₂-purged** keg (beer-out to beer-out) with a **clear hose**.
3. Push beer over, watching clarity in the hose (**backlight with a flashlight**).
4. **Stop the moment haze appears** — the target keg comes out very clear.

Also used for fining-in-keg, eisbock (partial freezing), and **transporting a keg to a party** without cloudy beer.

## Fining Agents (more aggressive)

Choose by what you're removing (yeast, proteins, tannins):

| Agent | Use |
|-------|-----|
| **Isinglass** | Traditional yeast removal in **ales** / cask |
| **Gelatin** | Yeast removal in **lagers** (works at cooler temps) |
| **Biofine Clear** | Silicate liquid — add while racking to secondary |
| **Polyclar (PVPP)** | Removes **tannins** (not food-safe — rack off after) |
| **Super-Kleer K.C.** | Two-stage (kieselsol + chitosan) |
| **Sparkolloid, bentonite** | Mead/wine finings, also work on beer |

**Tips:** time, gravity, and **cold** all help finings pack down (less loss). Some are "fluffy" (**Sparkolloid** especially) and settle slowly / rouse easily. **Be gentle, rack cleanly, expect some volume loss, and don't overdose** — excess finings strip body, color, and flavor.

## Fine vs Filter

Strong **prefers fining** — filtering is mechanically abusive, needs lots of setup, and strips body/color/flavor. He makes clear beer through good practice and only clarifies **competition-class** beer.

**His routine today:** mostly let nature work + the two-keg jumper after long cold rest. For pale/clarity-critical beers (Kölsch) or powdery yeast → **Biofine Clear or Super-Kleer K.C.**; coarse **plate filter** as a last resort. Gelatin on lagers near the end of lagering (when he remembers). For cask ales, the **Fuller's strain (WY1968 / WLP002)** is the most flocculent he's seen — just chill below **65°F (18°C)** and it drops fast.
