# Stabilizing

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Finishing Beer. Part of [[finishing-beer]].*

Stabilizing means **removing the proteins and tannins** that combine into hazes and shorten shelf life (chill haze).

## Most of the Work Happens Before Fermentation

Good brewing practice prevents most chill haze:

- **Mill grain properly**, get a good **hot break**, **rack off break material**, and **don't carry over trub**.
- **Copper finings** (kettle clarifiers) enhance protein/tannin removal — **Irish moss, Whirlfloc, or Breakbright**. See [[managing-the-boil]].

## Post-Fermentation Options

- **Silica gel** and **Polyclar** remove protein–tannin complexes; **Super-Kleer K.C.** is also effective.
- **Filtering while chill haze is present** is the final method — but it strips desired flavor and body.

> **If you don't have the problem, don't try to fix it.** Good practice + copper finings makes chill haze unlikely. If you do use post-fermentation finings, **rack off them before packaging**.
