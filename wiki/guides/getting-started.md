# Getting Started with BrewHub

BrewHub is a homebrew tracking app that helps you plan, brew, and review your batches.

## Creating Your First Batch

1. From the home page, click **New Batch**
2. Fill in the batch details: name, style, type (beer, cider, hop water, or other)
3. Add your fermentables, hops, and yeasts from your inventory
4. Set your equipment and water profile
5. Save and start brewing

## Equipment Setup

Before creating batches, set up your equipment in the **Inventory > Equipment** section. Key values:

- **Brewhouse efficiency** — how much sugar you extract (typically 70–80%)
- **Fermenter loss** — liquid left behind in the fermenter (trub, yeast cake)
- **Trub loss** — wort left in the kettle after transfer

## Inventory

Manage your ingredients in the Inventory section:

- **Fermentables** — base malts, caramel malts, adjuncts, sugars
- **Hops** — alpha acid percentage is required for IBU calculations
- **Cultures** — yeasts with attenuation and temperature range
- **Water profiles** — source and target mineral profiles
- **Kegs** — your keg inventory

## Brew Day

On the batch page, use the **Brew Day** section to record actual volumes, gravities, and temperatures as you brew.

## Fermentation Tracking

Log fermentation data points over time to track your fermentation curve.

## Tastings

Record structured tasting notes after your beer is ready, scoring appearance, aroma, flavor, mouthfeel, and overall impression.
