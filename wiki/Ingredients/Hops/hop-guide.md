# Hop Guide

Hops contribute bitterness, flavor, and aroma to beer. The timing, amount, and variety all affect the final result.

## Alpha Acids

Alpha acids (expressed as a percentage) are the primary source of bitterness. They isomerize during the boil into iso-alpha acids.

**IBU (International Bitterness Unit)** is the standard measure of perceived bitterness. A simple Tinseth approximation:

```
IBUs = (grams × alpha% × utilization) / volume_L
```

Utilization increases with boil time — longer boil = more bitterness.

## Hop Additions

| Addition | Timing | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| FWH (First Wort Hop) | Added to kettle before boil | Soft bitterness, some flavor |
| Boil | 60–90 min | Bitterness |
| Boil | 15–30 min | Flavor + some bitterness |
| Boil | 0–10 min | Flavor and aroma |
| Whirlpool / Hop Stand | Post-boil, 70–90°C | Aroma, soft bitterness |
| Dry Hop | Fermentation / conditioning | Aroma only |

## Hop Forms

- **Pellet** — most common, compact, good shelf life, easy to use
- **Leaf / Whole** — traditional, some brewers prefer for aroma
- **Cryo / Extract** — concentrated lupulin, high alpha/aroma

## Common Varieties

### Bittering
- **Magnum** — clean, smooth bitterness, neutral flavor
- **Columbus (CTZ)** — high alpha, dank, sometimes used for dual purpose

### Dual Purpose
- **Centennial** — floral, citrus
- **Chinook** — piney, grapefruit, spicy
- **Simcoe** — pine, passionfruit, earthy

### Aroma / Flavor
- **Citra** — intense citrus, tropical fruit
- **Mosaic** — complex fruit and earthiness
- **Galaxy** — passionfruit, peach, citrus
- **Hallertau Mittelfrüh** — noble, floral, herbal (lager)
- **Saaz** — spicy, herbal (Pilsner)

## Storage

Hops degrade over time. Store vacuum-sealed in the freezer. Check the harvest year and alpha acid on the package — old hops may have lower actual alpha than stated. For Gordon Strong's detailed storage method, see [[storing-hops]].
