# Mastering Ingredients

*Chapter 4 hub from Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer." The goal: categorize, characterize, differentiate, and **select** the five ingredient classes — malt, adjuncts, hops, yeast, water — so you can brew what you want and evaluate new products as they appear.*

A brewer needs a working understanding of ingredients to control the profile of the finished beer. You don't need to know *every* product — you need a reliable set you understand, plus a method for assessing new ones.

## The Five Classes

- **[[malt-overview]]** — germinated, kilned grain; the primary sugar source.
- **[[adjuncts-overview]]** — extra sugars: sugary (sugar, honey, syrups) and starchy (oats, corn, rye, wheat).
- **[[hops-overview]]** — bitterness, flavor, aroma, preservation. See also the practical [[hop-guide]].
- **[[choosing-a-yeast-strain]]** — the single biggest driver of fermentation character. See also the [[yeast-guide]].
- **[[water-philosophy]]** — mostly: get mash pH right and otherwise *leave it alone*.

## Assessing Ingredients — Sensory Method

There's no substitute for tasting. The goal is to **characterize** (describe) a profile and **differentiate** it from similar ingredients. Strong's golden rule for any experiment: **change only one variable at a time** — same wort for a yeast trial, same yeast for a hop trial, same water and fermentation conditions throughout.

| Ingredient | How to learn its character |
|------------|----------------------------|
| **Malt** | Chew it raw for a general impression; then brew a **single-malt beer** (low hops, neutral yeast). Steep crystal/roast malts at 150–170°F (66–77°C) for 30 min and taste the liquid. Cold-steep roasted malts — see [[handling-dark-grains]]. |
| **Sugars** | Taste raw, then as diluted syrup; ferment a sample with clean yeast for residual flavor. Or back-sweeten a neutral commercial beer right in the cup (dissolve dry sugar as a 1:1 simple syrup first). |
| **Starchy adjuncts** | Hard to judge without mashing; cook the food form (simmer in 2× water 15–20 min) for a general idea. |
| **Hops** | Crush and smell; steep 2 min in boiling water, chill, characterize aroma. For flavor, brew a single-hop test batch — **first wort hop** for max flavor, **knockout** addition for aroma. Judge whether bitterness is clean or harsh. |
| **Yeast** | Split one batch across multiple fermenters (growlers, soda bottles), same wort and conditions, different strains. A/B a new strain against a known one. |

> Do these as club or group experiments — more experienced palates extract more information. Always keep a **control** fermented in a known-good way so you can trust the data.
