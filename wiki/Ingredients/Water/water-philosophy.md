# Water — Strong's Philosophy

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Ingredients. Part of [[mastering-ingredients]]. For the deeper chemistry, see the Water & PH section: [[water-chemistry]], [[how-ph-affects-brewing]], [[salts]], [[residual-alkalinity]].*

Water is the most misunderstood brewing topic. Strong's blunt advice: **stop messing with your water so much.** Water adjustment is **advanced** and should be **one of the last things** you worry about — get mash pH right and otherwise leave it alone. *"If you only take away one thing from this book, stop messing around with your water."*

## What to Do at Each Skill Level

| Level | What you need to do |
|-------|---------------------|
| **New all-grain brewer** | Check three things: (1) Does it **taste/smell bad**? (2) Any **chlorine**? (3) **High carbonates**? "Yes" to any → fix it. Otherwise **just brew with it as-is** and see what you get. |
| **Moderately advanced** | Know your water's **calcium** and **pH**. Get a supplier analysis or send it out. Own a **calibrated pH meter**. Be comfortable replicating famous-city profiles if you like. |
| **Advanced** | Take more precise/frequent measurements and tighten process control — to **pinpoint** what needs tweaking, not to fiddle for its own sake. If you're already making great beer, you don't *need* to go beyond the moderate level. |

> The recurring warning: overadjusted beer "tastes like Alka-Seltzer." Precision without knowing your starting water is **confusing precision with accuracy**. Let your palate be the final judge.
