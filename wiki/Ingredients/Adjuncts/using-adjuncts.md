# Using Adjuncts — Strong's Approach

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Ingredients ("So What Do I Do?"). Part of [[adjuncts-overview]].*

## Practical Picks

- **Starchy adjuncts:** almost always **flaked** — mash alongside the other malts, no cereal mash. (Torrefied wheat occasionally, for its toasty note.)
- **Corn:** for extra sweetness / rounder flavor — light lagers, cream ale, classic American Pilsener, plus English bitter, mild, old ale, Flanders red, Irish red, blonde. ½–1 lb for character, up to **20–30%** for prominent corn character.
- **Rice:** Strong doesn't use it (little flavor) — reaches for white sugar instead.
- **Oats:** body and flavor. ¼ lb = a little body; 1 lb = quite chewy (great in imperial stout); **10–20%** in an oatmeal stout. **Toast the oats** for added complexity (a Radical Brewing tip).
- **Raw sugars:** turbinado, Demerara, muscovado (light→dark) work well in Belgian/English styles — ½–2 lb (227–907 g) per 5 gal. Jaggery is toffee-like; piloncillo is smoky/harsh (small amounts only). Find unusual sugars at international markets.
- **Plain sugar:** white beet sugar — to raise gravity and boost fermentability/attenuation (up to 20% in a tripel).
- **Honey:** ½–2 lb in blonde, APA, IPA (orange blossom is a favorite). ½ lb = background accent, more = noticeable. Dark honeys (buckwheat) for dark beers. **Don't waste varietal honeys** as a beer accent — make mead.
- **Belgian candi:** 1–2 lb of the **dark** versions (uses beet sugar in place of pale candi); prefers **liquid syrups** over rock candi.
- **Golden Syrup / treacle:** ½–1 can per 5 gal in British styles (mild, old ale, winter warmer) and some Belgians.
- **Molasses:** winter warmers and Christmas beers; **blackstrap sparingly** (1–4 Tbsp / 15–60 mL per 5 gal — very strong).

> **Read the label:** avoid "brown sugar" that's just molasses added to cane sugar — add them yourself if that's the goal. Least-processed = evaporated syrup.

## Recipe — Old Stranger Cask Ale (Old Ale, treacle + torrefied wheat)

Makes 5.5 gal (20.8 L) · OG 1.062 · FG 1.016 · 24 IBU · 6% ABV

- 10 lb (4.5 kg) Crisp Maris Otter · 1 lb (454 g) Crisp crystal 80°L · ¼ lb (113 g) roasted barley
- ¾ lb (340 g) CaraPils · ¾ lb (340 g) CaraMunich · 1 lb (454 g) torrefied wheat · 1 can (454 g) dark treacle
- 1.5 oz (42 g) Fuggles whole 5.3% AA @60 min · 0.5 oz (14 g) Sterling whole 6.2% AA @10 min · Wyeast 1098
- RO water + 1 tsp chalk. Mash 151°F (66°C) 2 h. Collect 8.5 gal, boil 90 min. Ferment 72°F (22°C). Prime with brown sugar + molasses, serve from cask.

## Recipe — Holiday Prowler (Christmas Ale, blackstrap molasses)

OG 1.058 · FG 1.014 · 19 IBU · 5.8% ABV — a best-of-show winner

- 9.5 lb (4.3 kg) Crisp Maris Otter · ¾ lb (340 g) Scotmalt crystal 40°L · ¼ lb (113 g) Crisp chocolate
- 1½ lb (680 g) clover honey · ½ can Lyle's Golden Syrup · ¼ cup blackstrap molasses
- 1 oz (28 g) Goldings whole 6.1% AA @60 min · 1 oz (28 g) Fuggles whole 4.3% AA @5 min · WLP002
- Spices: 4 cinnamon sticks, 1 nutmeg, 1 vanilla bean, 7 allspice, 1½ tsp whole cloves, 8 coriander seeds, peels from 2 nectarines
- Mash 158°F (70°C). 90-min boil. Steep chopped spices in a tight mesh bag at knockout 10 min, remove, chill fast. Ferment 68°F (20°C). Prime with muscovado, cask condition.
