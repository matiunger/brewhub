# Characterizing Malt — The Four Categories

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Mastering Ingredients. Part of [[malt-overview]].*

Strong groups all malts into four families by how they're made and used. This makes recipe formulation far simpler than tracking dozens of individual malts.

## 1. Base Malts

Enough enzymes to **self-convert**; usable up to **100% of the grist**.

- Two-row, Pilsner, pale ale, Vienna, Munich, **rauchmalz** (smoked), plus pale wheat/rye/oat malts.

## 2. Specialty / Character Malts

Used in **smaller percentages** for specific flavor — often just higher-kilned base malts. Often the primary flavor character of a beer.

- Aromatic/melanoidin, biscuit/Victory, Special Roast, honey malt, amber, brown malt.
- Usually mashed with the base malts, but **many can simply be steeped** for flavor.

## 3. Crystal / Caramel Malts

**Stewed** during kilning so starch converts to sugar *before* final kilning — **no mashing needed**, just liquefy and rinse the sugars in.

- A steep at **150–170°F (66–77°C)** does it — in the mash, at mash-out/vorlauf, or even in the boil.
- Anything labeled *crystal*, *caramel*, or European *cara-* . **Special "B"** is a very dark crystal with a unique **raisin** character. Crystal wheat and rye exist too.

## 4. Roasted Malts

Drum-roasted for dark color and sharp flavor — **no mashing needed**, treat like crystal.

- Chocolate, Kiln Coffee, black patent, Weyermann **Carafa**. **Roasted barley** is here too (technically unmalted).
- **Huskless / debittered** roasted malts (Carafa Special, Belgian debittered black) give color with **less acidity, tannin, harshness, and burnt flavor** — key to **black IPA, schwarzbier**, any dark-but-smooth style.
- **Weyermann Sinamar** is liquid Carafa Special II extract — add color + roast flavor any time from boil to packaging.

## Actionable Tips

- Want dark color *without* roast bite? Reach for the **huskless** versions — but use **more** to hit the same flavor level.
- **Blend** roasted malts (traditional + huskless) for layered, complex roast character.
- Manage harshness through **grain handling** ([[handling-dark-grains]]) and **water chemistry** ([[water-chemistry]]) as much as through malt selection.
