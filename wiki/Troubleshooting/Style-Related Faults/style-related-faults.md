# Style-Related Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[troubleshooting]].*

A beer can have **no obvious technical flaws yet still not seem right.** That's usually a **style-related fault** — about **balance and drinkability**, or how the beer compares against commercial examples, the style guidelines, or the taster's expectations.

These are **harder to identify** because they're subjective and measured against a (possibly arbitrary) standard. You need **style knowledge + structured tasting** to isolate and describe the gap. Style-related faults often trace back to **recipe formulation, ingredient selection, or process** — and the fixes overlap with the technical ones. See [[recipe-formulation]] and [[avoiding-clashing-flavors]].

## The Six Issues

- **[[low-gravity]]** — efficiency and boil-volume problems throwing off malt-to-hop balance.
- **[[incorrect-attenuation]]** — too dry or too sweet; how it shifts perceived bitterness.
- **[[boil-related-issues]]** — boil length/strength effects (DMS, volume, harshness, color).
- **[[harshness]]** — the four sources of hop-derived harshness (plus dark grain and alcohol).
- **[[aftertaste-issues]]** — the lasting impression: muddiness, grassiness, dryness, balance.
- **[[clarity]]** — diagnosing haze by its source (starch, sugar, yeast, protein/tannin).
