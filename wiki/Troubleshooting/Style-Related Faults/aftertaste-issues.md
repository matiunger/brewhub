# Aftertaste Issues

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[style-related-faults]].*

The aftertaste is the **lasting impression** — the flavor left after the beer is gone. Harshness and attenuation affect it (see [[harshness]] and [[incorrect-attenuation]]), but there are other problem areas:

| Aftertaste problem | Likely cause / fix |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Muddy, dull, indistinct** | Too many ingredients (**flavor clashes**), old/low-quality ingredients, or **high pH (>4.5)**. See [[avoiding-clashing-flavors]] and [[adjusting-finished-beer-ph]]. |
| **Lingering grassy / vegetal** | From **dry hopping** — switch to knockout / whirlpool / hopback, or dry hop **less than a week**. See [[whirlpooling]]. |
| **Not dry enough** | May not be attenuation alone — could be **pH too high** or **too little sulfate**. Some styles (Irish/Scottish) add a touch of **roasted barley** for a dry finish. |

> **Balance drives drinkability:** bitterness + sweetness give a lingering **bittersweet** finish. **Excessive finishing sweetness or a full body makes a beer hard to drink**; lack of a **crisp finish** makes a beer a sipper rather than a drinker.
