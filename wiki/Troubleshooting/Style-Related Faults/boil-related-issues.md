# Boil-related Issues

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[style-related-faults]]. See also [[managing-the-boil]].*

Boil **length** and **strength** affect several things:

- **DMS:** if using Pilsner malt or other high-**SMM** malt, run a **90-minute boil** to drive off DMS. See [[hopping-mash-and-boil-faults]].
- **Evaporation & final volume:** boil strength sets the evaporation rate and final volume. Miss the **volume target** and your **OG and BU:GU** drift, changing the balance. See [[low-gravity]].
- **Long boils (>90 min):** extract **more harshness** (and bitterness) from hops — so **add hops only in the last 90 minutes or less**. Long boils also **deepen color** and add **caramelized** flavors — desirable in some styles, not others. **Don't use a long boil unless you want these side effects.**
