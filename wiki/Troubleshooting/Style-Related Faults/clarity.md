# Clarity

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[style-related-faults]]. Removal techniques live in [[clarifying]] and [[stabilizing]] (Finishing Beer).*

A cloudy beer can be off-putting and can even affect body and flavor. **First diagnose the source of the haze:**

| Haze source | Indicates |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Starch** | A **mashing problem** (incomplete conversion) |
| **Sugar** | An **incomplete fermentation** |
| **Yeast** | A **flocculation** problem |
| **Protein / tannin** | Poor **hot/cold break**, or carrying over **break material** to the fermenter |

> **Avoiding cloudiness beats removing it later.** Convert starches fully, get good **hot and cold break**, fully ferment the sugars, and let yeast **settle** properly.

## Fixes

- **Finings** in the boil or post-fermentation. See [[stabilizing]] and [[clarifying]].
- **Cold + time** flocculate particulates and improve clarity.
- **Filtering** is a rougher last resort.
- **Handle clarity before packaging** — removing haze gets harder once the beer is **carbonated**. See [[adjusting-clarity]].
