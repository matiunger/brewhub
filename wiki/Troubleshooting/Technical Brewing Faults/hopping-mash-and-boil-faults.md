# Hopping, Mash & Boil Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[technical-brewing-faults]]. Faults from tannins, hops, malt, and the boil.*

## Astringent

**Perception:** mouth-**puckering**, lingering bitter harshness, **husky/grainy**. Primarily a mouthfeel from **tannins** — control tannin extraction to control astringency.

**Control — grain husks:** **don't oversparge**, **don't overcrush** (husk fines = more surface area), **don't boil grain**, **don't sparge above 170°F (77°C) or pH 6**, use fewer dark grains (esp. black malt). **Hop tannins:** less whole/high-alpha hops, **lower-sulfate water** (sulfate accentuates tannin harshness). Avoid raw spices, fruit **pith and skins**. See [[preparing-to-sparge]] and [[handling-dark-grains]].

## DMS (dimethyl sulfide)

**Perception:** creamed/cooked **corn**, cooked (sulfury) vegetables. From malt **SMM** — paler malts have more (highly kilned malts have less). Above ~149–158°F (65–70°C) SMM → DMS, but DMS is volatile and **boils off**.

**Control:** reduce SMM (less Pilsner/very pale malt); **drive off DMS with a long, vigorous, open, rolling boil**. DMS lingers from a weak/short/**covered** boil, or forms **after** the boil from **long whirlpools / slow chilling** — so **cool fast**. Can also come from **infection** → healthy starter, sanitation, quick chill + pitch. See [[managing-the-boil]] and [[whirlpooling]].

## Grassy

**Perception:** fresh-cut grass / green leaves. A **hop-derived** character from prolonged contact with hop vegetal matter or **raw hops** (dry hopping).

**Control:** reduce/eliminate **dry hopping** (substitute another late-hop technique), use fewer whole hops overall, **avoid post-fermentation oxygen** (oxidized hops turn harsh/grassy), and check hop/malt **freshness** (staleness can read grassy). See [[hopping]] and [[storing-hops]].

## Vegetal

**Perception:** cooked/canned/rotten vegetables (cabbage, celery, onion, asparagus, parsnip). From **DMS**, **wort-spoilage infection**, or **oxidized ingredients**.

**Control:** fast, vigorous fermentation (**healthy active starter** → short lag, the usual cause is pre-fermentation bacterial contamination); sanitation; check for **stale/old ingredients** (esp. old liquid malt extract — a "tangy vegetal" flavor); **avoid oversparging at low temperatures**.
