# Infection & Phenolic Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[technical-brewing-faults]]. Faults from contamination, chlorophenols, and phenolic compounds. Most are prevented by good sanitation — see [[cleaning]].*

## Medicinal (chlorophenolic)

**Perception:** Chloroseptic lozenges, disinfectant, harsh medicine/cleaner — **one of the most offensive beer flavors.** From **chlorine + phenols → chlorophenols**.

**Control — remove chlorine:** avoid chlorine/chloramine water (use RO if needed), filter with **activated charcoal**, neutralize chloramines with **Campden**, and **never use chlorine bleach sanitizers**. **Reduce phenols:** cut grain-husk/astringency sources, avoid excessive whole hops, good sanitation (wild yeast), avoid phenol-producing strains (weizen, some Belgian). See [[adjusting-water]].

## Plastic (phenolic)

**Perception:** adhesive bandages, electrical tape, styrene. Usually a **wild-yeast infection**, or some strains fermented **very hot**.

**Control:** sanitation; different/fresher yeast (old dry yeast can do this); healthy active yeast, short lag time, **lower fermentation temperature**.

## Smoky (phenolic)

**Perception:** smoke, charcoal, burnt. From infection, burnt ingredients, or scorching.

**Control:** sanitation; lower-phenol strain; adjust fermentation temp; **check for a scorched mash/boil** — clean residue, **stir decoctions constantly**, control heat on kettles/tuns (see [[managing-heat]]); reduce dark malts or rauchmalz.

## Spicy (phenolic)

**Perception:** clove, pepper, vanilla — **often positive** phenols from yeast, ingredients, or process.

**Control:** certain strains/hops give clove/pepper; **adjust fermentation temperature** (up or down by strain/style); **oak aging** boosts vanilla.

## Sour / Acidic

**Perception:** a basic taste, also aroma/mouthfeel. **Lactic** = clean sour, **citric** = sharp sour, **acetic** = harsh vinegar (see Vinegary).

**Control:** sanitation; check equipment for **nicks/scratches** harboring bacteria; **segregate equipment** if you brew lambics/sour beers; different strain; **don't hold a long low-temperature mash** (that's a sour mash).

## Vinegary (acetic)

**Perception:** vinegar sourness. From **Acetobacter** — which **needs oxygen** (aerobic).

**Control:** sanitation **and avoid oxygen uptake**; check equipment for nicks/scratches; segregate sour-beer equipment; different strain; **avoid barrels / wood** in the beer. See [[beer-stability]].

## Musty

**Perception:** stale, moldy cellar; age, filth, dirt. From mold/stale ingredients, oxidation, or infection.

**Control:** avoid oxidation; good sanitation; **avoid peat-smoked malt**; check/charcoal-filter water (or RO); use fresh malt and hops; avoid corks ("corked"); check caps/supplies for contamination.
