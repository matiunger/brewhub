# Packaging & Water Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[technical-brewing-faults]]. Faults from light, oxygen, water, and equipment.*

## Light-struck (skunky)

**Perception:** **skunky / catty.** Visible light hits hop compounds and forms **mercaptans** — the same chemical skunks use. Humans detect it at **parts per billion**.

**Control:** keep **direct sunlight** off the beer once hops are added; store in **brown bottles** (clear/green are up to **8× less** effective) — but brown isn't opaque, so still **limit sunlight/fluorescent exposure**. Some varieties (e.g. **Cluster**) are naturally catty — avoid them in late additions.

## Oxidized

**Perception:** stale, **papery, wet cardboard** — this form is **always bad and not recoverable.** (Note: *slow* oxidation can be desirable — **sherry**-like in barleywines/old ales; softened tannins in hoppy beers; some aging just turns beer sweeter/caramelly and more estery before going dull and stale.)

**Control:** **no oxygen from the second half of fermentation onward.** Don't let airlocks run dry; **don't splash** when racking/bottling; **blanket with CO₂** after racking; check cap/keg-seal fit; **oxygen-scavenging caps**; **purge bottles/kegs with CO₂**; **bottle-condition** (yeast takes up oxygen); **store cold**; **drink fresh.** See [[beer-stability]].

## Metallic

**Perception:** iron, copper, coins, blood (flavor; aroma at high levels). From **water impurities** or **equipment** defects, or chemical additions.

**Control:** test water for metallic ions (esp. **iron**); reduce salt additions; check equipment for **rust**; ensure stainless is **passivated**; rinse sanitizers fully; try **RO water** and add only necessary salts. See [[water-chemistry]] and [[salts]].
