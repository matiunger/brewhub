# Fermentation Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[technical-brewing-faults]]. Faults driven by yeast and fermentation conditions. See also [[managing-fermentation]] and [[choosing-a-yeast-strain]].*

## Acetaldehyde

**Perception:** fresh-cut **green apple**; grassy, rough, immature ("green beer"). An intermediate that rises during fermentation then falls as beer conditions — a **maturity marker**.

**Control:** proper fermentation (pitch rate, oxygen, nutrients) and **conditioning in contact with healthy yeast** until fully attenuated. Reduce head pressure to blow off the volatile aldehydes; warm conditioning and higher yeast concentration help. **Avoid packaging oxygen** (alcohol oxidizes back to acetaldehyde → stale; light/heat catalyze this). Some strains produce more — switch strains.

## Alcoholic / Hot

**Perception:** spicy, vinous, warming → **hot/burning** at high levels; adds bitterness. Ethanol itself isn't a flaw unless **unbalanced or under-aged**, but it travels with fusels (see **Solvent / Fusel** below).

**Control:** **reduce alcohol** (lower OG, raise mash temp / less sugary adjuncts, less-attenuative yeast). **Cleaner fermentation** — lower temperature, healthy yeast, good conditions, good sanitation (infection raises attenuation/alcohol). **Age longer** — big beers need cellaring to smooth out.

## Diacetyl

**Perception:** **butter, butterscotch, movie-theater popcorn**; slick mouthfeel. A yeast by-product normally reduced during fermentation.

**Control:** good fermentation practice (pitch rate, aeration, nutrients; avoid low-nitrogen adjuncts). **Don't remove yeast prematurely** — no early crash-cool, rack, filter, or fine; rest on the yeast until fully attenuated. Remove via a **diacetyl rest** (raise temp / extend secondary). Bacteria, wild/mutated yeast also produce it → sanitation. Some strains are known producers. Bottle-conditioning at cellar temp gives more reduction time. See [[kraeusening]].

## Estery

**Perception:** **fruity** — strawberry, pear, banana, apple, grape, citrus; **solventy** at excess. Wanted in many English/Belgian ales, unwanted in most lagers.

**Increase esters:** estery/flocculent strain, warm fermentation, low oxygen, high gravity, low fatty acids, more zinc, **shallow fermenters / lower pressure / less stirring**, generally **stressing the yeast**. **Decrease esters:** lager strains, cool fermentation, growth factors (oxygen, lipids, stirring), **high CO₂ pressure / tall cylindrical tanks**, longer cellar aging on the yeast. See [[managing-fermentation]] (fermenter geometry).

## Solvent / Fusel

**Perception:** **hot burning** palate; alcoholic, spicy, vinous, solventy; can cause **temple headaches**. Higher alcohols are more aromatic than ethanol; produced when rapid yeast growth demands more amino acids than the wort supplies.

**Increases with:** high dissolved oxygen, tall-fermenter convection/stirring, **high temperature**, high gravity (>13 °P / 1.052), **low pitch rate**, low-pressure fermentation, certain (often ale) strains, glucose/no-nitrogen adjuncts ("hello, malt liquor").

**Control:** slow yeast growth — **lower temperature**, different strain; **raise fermenter pressure** for big/warm batches (also curbs esters); good sanitation. Age to esterify/smooth.

## Sulfury

**Perception:** **rotten eggs (H₂S)** or **burning match (SO₂)**. SO₂ is common/positive in European lagers (antioxidant, antimicrobial); H₂S comes from **unhealthy yeast / nutrient deficiency** and volatilizes easily.

**Control:** **Lower SO₂** — warmer conditioning, longer lagering, less head pressure; reduce formation with more nutrients/lipids/aeration, healthy yeast, and removing hot/cold break. **Reduce H₂S** — higher lagering temp, taller fermenter (blows off faster); check for infection. **Water sulfate** (gypsum/Burton salts) reads as sulfur — ease off. Some (lager) strains produce more → switch. **Copper in the boil** scavenges sulfur (Strong's false bottom is copper) — but **never add copper to finished beer**.

## Yeasty / Autolysis

**Perception:** bready, sulfury, yeastlike (**young yeast** still in suspension) — or **glutamate/umami** (soy, mushroom, Parmesan) from **autolysis** (beer held too long/warm on the yeast). Autolysis also **raises pH** after fermentation completes — a confirmation.

**Control:** young-yeast character → more flocculent strain, time to flocculate, fining/filtering, carry over less yeast, age longer. **Autolysis** → rack off the yeast **as soon as** green flavors are reduced; don't store long/warm on the cake; rack to secondary if more conditioning is needed. See [[conditioning]] and [[clarifying]].
