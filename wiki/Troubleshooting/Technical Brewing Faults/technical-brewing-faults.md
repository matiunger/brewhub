# Technical Brewing Faults

*From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer" — Troubleshooting. Part of [[troubleshooting]]. (Elaborated from material Strong helped develop for the BJCP.)*

Technical faults are what most people picture as "flaws" — single faults or combinations derived from the brewing **process**, often from **fermentation**. Basic tasting and QC catch many of them. **Sensory thresholds vary by individual** (genetics, perception, insensitivity) — know your own limitations.

> **New brewers' beers often show clusters of faults** from weak fermentations: off-flavors, poor attenuation, muddy/vegetal notes. A slow start lets spoilage bacteria grow; weak fermentation throws off by-products the yeast can't clean up. Poor ingredients, oxidation, long lag times, and bad handling all pile up.
