# Troubleshooting

*Chapter 7 hub from Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer." After you've brewed, you're not done — check the beer, fix what you can, and learn from the rest. Troubleshooting is a problem-solving discipline requiring deep knowledge of your system and processes.*

> *"Life. Don't talk to me about life."* — Marvin

As the brewer you have an advantage judges don't: **perfect information** about your recipe and process. The questions are simply: how do you **identify** a fault, and what can you **do** about it — on the fly, after fermentation, or (if unsalvageable) accept as a learning experience?

## Most Faults Are Avoidable

Strong's key control points — nail these and you avoid most **technical** faults:

- **Fresh, clean ingredients**
- **Good wort production** (proper FAN/yeast nutrients, proper maltose level)
- **Sufficient fresh, high-quality yeast** with proper aeration
- **Controlled fermentation conditions**
- **No oxygen on the cold side**
- **Good sanitation** — see [[cleaning]]

## Detecting Beer Faults

Treat faults like a **doctor treats illness**: symptom → diagnosis → treatment of the *underlying* problem. **Proper diagnosis is critical** — randomly applying "fixes" without knowing the cause usually makes things worse. Use **structured tasting** to isolate the fault, then consult the fault lists to find cause and effect.

### Temporary vs Permanent

- **Often fade with proper aging** — too bitter, too roasty, too estery, too alcoholic.
- **Here to stay → dump it** — sour, medicinal, oxidized.
- **Don't write off a batch** unless you know it has an unrecoverable fault.

### Diagnostic Tips

- **Some faults masquerade as positives** — caramel + fruitiness can be an English beer *or* early oxidation; heavy kettle caramelization is confused with diacetyl. Look for **confirming evidence** before concluding.
- **Balance faults are temperature-dependent** — a too-cold bitter beer reads more bitter (malt suppressed); warmth exaggerates esters/alcohol. **Assess at proper serving temperature.**
- **Use your insider knowledge** — sluggish fermentation? missed gravity? Prune the possibilities. Check the "medical history": do other batches with the same ingredients/process share the problem? Recent changes? Recurring issue?
- **For subjective faults, get broader input** from other brewers, tasters, judges — are observations consistent?
