# Lautering

*Mastering Techniques — separating the wort. From Gordon Strong, "Brewing Better Beer."*

Lautering is the process that **filters sweet wort from spent grain** — the "straining" or "separating" step. It is *not* the same as **sparging**, which is the *rinsing* of the grain. Sparging is often part of lautering but is not always required.

## Key Takeaways

- Lautering needs a vessel with a **false bottom or screen** (a dedicated lauter tun, or a mash tun doing double duty). This is the one piece of equipment where a false bottom is essential.
- It happens **after the mash is complete**, the sugar composition is fixed, and the mash is at mash-out temperature (typically **168–170°F / 76–77°C**).
- **Vorlauf** (recirculate the cloudy wort back through the grain bed until it runs clear) to establish the grain bed as a filter.

## Actionable Tips

- Perform the vorlauf **gently, without splashing** — oxygen uptake here makes the beer lose its peak flavors prematurely.
- Hit **mash-out temperature before lautering** to fix the wort and help sugars dissolve.
